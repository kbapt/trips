Perfect! For an 8 and 10-year-old, the Lesse Valley has some great options. Here's a weekend itinerary I'd recommend:

Saturday: Domaine des Grottes de Han

The Caves of Han are a perfect family adventure! Your kids will love:

  • Guided tour through spectacular underground caves with impressive stalactites
  • Wildlife park with European animals in their natural habitat
  • PrehistoHan museum with archaeological discoveries

The tour takes about 2 hours and is suitable for children. Comfortable shoes are recommended as there is some walking involved.

Family ticket (2+2): €110 9:30 AM - 2:30 PM

Sunday: Guided Hike & Picnic in Lesse Valley

Explore the beautiful Lesse Valley with a family-friendly guided hike:

  • Easy 5 km hike suitable for children with wonderful views
  • Local guide sharing stories about the region's nature and history
  • Gourmet picnic featuring local specialties
  • Kids' nature scavenger hunt with small prizes

The entire experience takes about 4 hours. Dress in layers and bring sun protection.

Family experience: €90 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM

This sounds perfect! I have a few questions though. Is the cave tour accessible for children? And how difficult is the hike?

Trip Planner

Great questions! Here are more details:

Cave tour accessibility: The Grottes de Han tour is definitely suitable for children aged 8 and 10. The cave paths are well-maintained with good lighting and handrails. The standard tour is about 1.5 km long and takes approximately 2 hours. There are a few steeper sections with stairs (about 120 steps in total), but they have regular rest stops. The temperature inside is around 13°C year-round, so light jackets are recommended.

Hike difficulty: The Lesse Valley hike I've suggested is specifically chosen to be family-friendly. It's classified as easy to moderate with minimal elevation gain (about 100 metres). The 5 km trail follows well-marked paths through forest and meadows with beautiful viewpoints of the valley. The pace is relaxed with plenty of stops for the guide to share interesting facts and for the picnic. Most 8 and 10-year-olds find it very manageable, and the scavenger hunt activities keep them engaged throughout the hike.
