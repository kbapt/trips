Traditional Portuguese Pottery Workshop

About This Experience

Learn the art of traditional Portuguese pottery in this hands-on workshop led by master artisan João Silva, whose family has been creating azulejos and ceramic pieces for over five generations.

Working from his historic studio in the Alfama district, João will guide you through the entire process from preparing clay to glazing techniques that have remained unchanged for centuries. You'll learn about the cultural significance of Portuguese ceramic arts and discover the symbols and patterns that make this tradition unique.

By the end of this 3-hour session, you'll create your own hand-painted tile and a small ceramic piece to take home as a meaningful souvenir of your experience.

What's Included

  • All materials and tools
  • Firing of your pieces (can be shipped to your home address)
  • Traditional Portuguese refreshments
  • Historical guide to Portuguese pottery

Your Host: João Silva

João is a 5th generation potter who has been working with clay since childhood. His work has been featured in national museums and he has received recognition as a Master Craftsman by the Portuguese Ministry of Culture.

"I'm passionate about keeping our ceramic traditions alive and sharing the stories behind each pattern and technique with visitors from around the world."

Getting There

Location & Transportation

The workshop is located in the historic Alfama district at Rua dos Artesãos, 27, Lisbon.

Public Transit

Take tram 28 to Alfama stop, then walk 5 minutes east.

Taxi/Uber

Approximately €8-10 from central Lisbon.

Parking

Limited street parking. Public parking garage available at Praça do Comércio (800m away).
